Website Planning Document

1. Site Name

The Betterlink Systems brand, as shown in the logo, represents the company's mission of creating better connections between educational institutions, local authorities, and their stakeholders through innovative technological solutions. Operating as "Betterlink Systems" (trading as from Betterlink Industries), the name conveys:

  • Better - Emphasizing the improvement and optimization our systems provide
  • Link - Highlighting the connectivity and integration aspects of our solutions
  • Systems - Directly stating our focus on comprehensive technological systems

A potential domain name would be betterlinksystems.com, which is straightforward, memorable, and clearly represents the company's identity.

2. Site Purpose

The Betterlink Systems website serves as a comprehensive digital platform to showcase our specialized solutions for educational institutions and local authorities. The site aims to:

  • Establish Betterlink Systems as an industry leader in educational technology and ERP solutions
  • Clearly communicate our service offerings and their benefits to potential clients
  • Demonstrate our expertise through case studies, testimonials, and technical insights
  • Provide interactive demonstrations of our systems' capabilities
  • Generate qualified leads through strategic calls-to-action and contact forms
  • Offer valuable educational content through our blog to establish thought leadership
  • Create a user-friendly platform for existing clients to access support resources

The website will be designed to cater to decision-makers in educational institutions and local government bodies who are looking for efficient, reliable, and innovative technological solutions to streamline their operations.

3. Scenarios

Target Audience Scenarios:

School Administrator Scenario: "Our school district is struggling with managing student data across multiple platforms. What specific educational management systems does Betterlink offer that could help us consolidate our data and improve administrative efficiency?"

Local Authority IT Director Scenario: "We're looking to upgrade our outdated ERP system for our municipality. Can I see examples of successful implementations Betterlink has completed for similar local authorities, and what ROI they achieved?"

University Technology Officer Scenario: "We need a system that can integrate with our existing infrastructure. How customizable are Betterlink's solutions, and what is the implementation process like for higher education institutions?"

School District Finance Director Scenario: "What kind of support and training does Betterlink provide after system implementation? We need to ensure our staff can effectively use any new technology we invest in."

4. Color Schema

The color scheme for Betterlink Systems reflects professionalism, trust, and innovation while maintaining accessibility standards:

#000000
Primary Color
#E74C18
Secondary Color
#C41200
Accent Color
#F5F7FA
Background Color
#333333
Text Color

Color Usage:

  • Primary Color (#000000): Will be used for the main navigation, headings, and text elements to match the black elements in the logo and establish brand identity.
  • Secondary Color (#E74C18): Will be applied to secondary elements, hover states, icons, and subtle highlights to match the bright orange flame in the logo.
  • Accent Color (#C41200): Reserved for call-to-action buttons, important highlights, and elements requiring immediate attention to match the deep red elements in the logo.
  • Background Color (#F5F7FA): Used for page backgrounds to create a clean, professional look while providing sufficient contrast with text.
  • Text Color (#333333): Used for body text to ensure readability while being slightly softer than pure black.

Roboto will be used for all body text, navigation items, buttons, and general interface elements. Its excellent readability at various sizes makes it perfect for detailed information and instructions.

Font Usage:

  • Montserrat (Bold): Main headings (h1), page titles
  • Montserrat (Medium): Subheadings (h2, h3)
  • Roboto (Regular): Body text, paragraphs, lists
  • Roboto (Light): Captions, secondary information
  • Roboto (Bold): Emphasis, navigation items, buttons
